    • Basic rules, basic violations and basic fouls

    As with any sports league, there are quite a lot of rules, violations and fouls that both casual and hardcore fans will know and argue about. Here are a few.

    5 basic rules

    1. Only 5 players on the court per team at all times. More than 5 will result in a technical foul.
    2. Any team only has 5 seconds to inbound the ball. If they fail to do so, this will result in a turnover.
    3. Each team only has 8 seconds per play to bring the ball past the halfcourt line. A turnover will happen if they fail to do so.
    4. Each team has a total of 6 timeouts.
    5. Each team has 24 seconds per play to shoot the ball. If the ball hits the net/backboard and the same team regains possesion, the shot clock resets to 11 seconds.

    3 basic violations

    1. If a player holds the ball with two hands then dribbles again this is called as a double dribble.
    2. If a player takes more than two steps without dribbling the ball, this is called as a travelling violation.
    4. If the ball goes past the court and onto the differently coloured part of the court ( usually black ) it is called as a out of bounds violation.

    3 basic fouls

    1. If a player commits illegal contact towards another player when they are in the act of shooting this is called as a shooting foul. Free throws are awarded based on if the player was behind or in front of the three point line and if they made the shot or not.
    2. If a player commits illegal contact towards another player when they have the ball but are not in the act of shooting, this is called as a reach in foul. After a certain point these also become free throws.
    3. If a player displays unsportsmanlike conduct towards the referee or to another player ( getting into a fight, excessive complaining and taunting ) this is called as a technical foul. 2 technicals result in an ejection. The other team shoots a free throw and regains possesion of ball.

      4. The positions

      There are a total of 5 positions in an NBA game.

      Each of these positions have there own set of skills that a player must live up to.

      These positions are:

      1. The point guard
      2. The shooting guard
      3. The small forward
      4. The power forward
      5. The center

      The point guard

      The point guard is arguably the most important position out of the 5.

      The point guard is responsible for getting the offense going, throwing the most passes and being the vocal leader of the team normally.

      With a good point guard a team may go from barely missing the playoffs to becoming title contenders.

      Most times the point guard is the shortest or among the shortest on the team standing at about 5'10 to 6'3.

      Some notable point guards are:

      1. Stephen Curry
      2. Luka Doncic
      3. Trae Young
      4. Chris Paul
      5. Fred Van Vleet

      The Shooting guard

      The shooting guard is usually the best scorer or shooter on the team.

      These are the players that are often hanging around the three point line on offense.

      They are also the players that might drop 40 on any given night.

      While this position isn't as vital as a point guard, it is still incredibly important.

      Some notable shooting guards are:

      1. James Harden
      2. Bradley Beal
      3. Klay Thompson
      4. Devin Booker
      5. Gary Trent Jr.
        6. 25 best shooting guards of last season

      The small forward

      The small forward is usually the most balanced player out of the 5.

      While the first two positions are usually played by average or good defensive players and great to exceptional offensive players.

      The small forward position is often played by players that can be really great on both sides.

      These players are known to guard the bigger positions and are also known to have a great deal of strength and muscle.

      Some notable small forwards are:

      1. Lebron James
      2. Kevin Durant
      3. Kawhi Leonard
      4. Jimmy Butler
      5. Og Anunoby
        6. Best small forwards of last season

      The power forward

      The power forward is much alike to the aformentioned small forward and soon to be mentioned center.

      While this position's players aren't always the greatest offensive threat, they are very effective at defending and collecting rebounds.

      Infact, most power forwards responsiblity is the play defense first and offense later. This doesn't mean that they are bad at offense though.

      Some notable power forwards are:

      1. Giannis Antentokounmpo
      2. Pascal Siakam
      3. Anthony Davis
      4. Karl Anthony Towns
      5. Scottie Barnes
        6. All 30 power forwards ranked

      The center

      The centers are almost always the biggest and most strongest player on the court.

      Most times, defense is their main and only focus. There are of course outliers though.

      The center is likely the second most important player on the team behind the point guard.

      Some notable centers are:

      1. Nikola Jokic
      2. Joel Embiid
      3. Rudy Gobert
      4. Bam Adebayo

      The Awards

      Finally, the awards. One of the many things that define how good a player was in a certain season.

      MVP

      Starting off with the most popular, the MVP award stands for Most Valuable Player and is often awarded to the best player in the league. The most recent MVP was Nikola Jokic.

      ROTY

      ROTY stands for Rookie of the Year. It is awarded to the best first year player of that certain year. The most recent recipient was Scottie Barnes

      DPOY

      DPOY stands for Defensive player of the Year. It is awarded to the best defensive player of that certain year. The most recent recipient was Marcus Smart

      6MOTY

      6MOTY stands for 6th man of the year. It is awarded to the best bench player of that year. The most recent recipient was Tyler Herro

      MIP

      MIP stands for Most Improved Player. It is awarded to the player that has made the biggest improvement from 1 season to another. The most recent recipient was Ja Morant

      COTY

      COTY stands for Coach of the Year. It is awarded to the best coach of any team of that certain year. The most recent recipient was Monty Williams
